# Autonomous & Robotic Mobility

**Track:** [[mobility]]

## Description

Compliance and operational tooling for the first wave of autonomous and remotely operated vehicles on German public roads. Three distinct regulatory regimes have activated in 2025–2026: the AFGBV (Autonomous Driving Approval and Operation Ordinance), which allows Level 4 vehicles in designated operating areas; the StVFernLV (Remote Control Ordinance), in force 1 December 2025, which regulates teleoperators and their qualifications; and the §70 StVO Ausnahmegenehmigung regime, which governs sidewalk delivery robots city by city. Each creates certification, logging, and reporting obligations for which no off-the-shelf software exists. A fourth wave — drone and u-space compliance under EU Regulation 2023/1769 — is emerging in parallel.

## Key Regulations

| Regulation | What it requires | In force |
|---|---|---|
| AFGBV (Autonome-Fahrzeuge-Genehmigungs-und-Betriebs-Verordnung) | KBA type approval, designated operating areas, Technische Aufsicht certification for Level 4 AV | Since July 2022; active deployments 2025+ |
| StVFernLV (Straßenverkehrs-Fernlenkungsverordnung) | Type approval, teleoperator certification, KBA Betriebserlaubnis, and session logging for remotely controlled vehicles | Since 1 December 2025 |
| §70 StVO (Ausnahmegenehmigung) | City-by-city permit for sidewalk delivery robots; no national framework yet | Current legal basis; national ordinance expected 2026–2027 |
| EU U-Space Regulation (2021/664 + 2023/1769) | Designated airspace management for drone operations; requires digital flight authorisation | Since 26 January 2023 |
| Drohnen-Verordnung (LuftVO) | German implementing rules for drone category classifications and registration | Ongoing |

## Market Size

- **StVFernLV / teleoperation:** ~20 active teleoperations companies in Germany today; ~100 expected by 2027. At €24k average ACV and 50% penetration: SAM €1.2m now, growing with deployment scale.
- **Delivery robots (§70 StVO):** ~10 active operators in Germany; TAM grows substantially when a national robot ordinance creates a registration market for thousands of devices.
- **AFGBV / Level 4 AV:** first commercial deployments in 2025–2026; compliance tooling TAM scales with deployment density — total European AV market projected at €100bn+ by 2035.
- **U-space / drones:** European commercial drone market projected at €14bn by 2030; German u-space compliance is an addressable slice.

## Best-in-Class Benchmarks

- **Waymo (US)** — sets the operational and compliance documentation standard for Level 4 AV programmes; German AFGBV is modelled partly on the US NHTSA framework.
- **KBA (Kraftfahrt-Bundesamt)** — the German type-approval authority; their Betriebserlaubnis process for AFGBV and StVFernLV defines what compliant documentation looks like.
- **Fernride GmbH** — the leading German teleoperated truck company; the first organisation actively navigating StVFernLV obligations and the reference customer for this pillar.
- **DLR u-space prototype corridors** — DLR's research defines the technical baseline for drone traffic management in German airspace.
