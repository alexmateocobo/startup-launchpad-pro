# Consumer Rights Awareness and Enforcement

**Track:** [[legal]]

## Description

Evidence-grade tooling for consumer NGOs, qualified entities, and regulators enforcing the Digital Services Act (DSA) and Unfair Commercial Practices Directive (UCPD) against manipulative digital design and deceptive commercial practices. The bottleneck is not the law — DSA Article 25 and UCPD prohibit dark patterns clearly — but enforcement capacity: Verbraucherzentralen and the Bundesnetzagentur DSA team must manually document violations before filing. First national DSA enforcement decisions are expected in 2026, creating urgent demand for reproducible, legally defensible evidence packages.

## Key Regulations

| Regulation | What it requires | In force |
|---|---|---|
| DSA Article 25 | Prohibits dark patterns and manipulative interface design for platforms | Since 17 February 2024 |
| UCPD (Unfair Commercial Practices Directive) | Prohibits misleading and aggressive commercial practices | Ongoing |
| VDuG Abhilfeklage | Collective redress mechanism for qualified entities | Since 2023 |
| EU Dark Patterns Guidelines | Commission interpretive guidance on DSA Article 25 | Published late 2025 |
| Digital Fairness Act | Anticipated additional restrictions on subscription traps and manipulative design | Expected 2026 |
| ePrivacy Directive | Governs cookie consent dark patterns | Ongoing |
