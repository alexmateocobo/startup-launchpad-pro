# AccessibilityMarketplace

**Pillar:** [[digital-accessibility-and-compliance]]
**One-liner:** Lived-experience accessibility tester marketplace: matches disabled testers (blind, low-vision, deaf, motor-impairment, cognitive) with Mittelstand and enterprise product teams for BFSG-compliant usability audits, creating paid work for an underemployed population.
**Shortlist score:** 24/35

## Problem

BFSG compliance requires not only automated WCAG checks but real-user testing by people with disabilities. The German disability community has an unemployment rate roughly 2-3x higher than the general population. Existing providers (Fable Tech, Access Works, Pfennigparade) are either non-German, project-based, or supply-constrained. German product teams struggle to find, pay, and brief disabled testers at scale. The result: lots of tokenised one-off audits, no continuous feedback loop, and a wasted talent pool.

## Regulatory driver / Why now

- BFSG compels demonstrable user testing for in-scope services.
- Inclusion Deutschland campaigns drive public-awareness pressure.
- Gesetz zur Stärkung der Teilhabe am Arbeitsleben 2024 creates subsidies for companies hiring disabled workers or paying for services from inclusion companies.
- The Pfennigparade model (München-based large inclusion company) is scaling and provides a reference.

## Target users

- **Testers (supply side):** disabled professionals across blind, deaf, motor-impairment, cognitive categories.
- **Payers (demand side):**
  - Mittelstand and enterprise product teams.
  - Public-sector agencies under BITV 2.0.
  - Accessibility consultancies (via subcontracting).
  - Research and academic projects (DFG, BMBF funding).

## Solution

Two-sided marketplace:

1. Tester side: onboarding, categorisation by disability + tech skill, pay-per-session scheduling, payroll/Rechnung handling, training and growth path.
2. Buyer side: project briefs, automated tester matching, session recording, structured feedback reports (WCAG-mapped).
3. Continuous programme option: subscribed buyers get a recurring panel.
4. Integration with BFSG compliance tools (e.g. BFSGPilot).

## Business model

- **Commission:** 20-30% of tester fees.
- **Continuous panel subscription:** €3-15k/month per buyer.
- **Enterprise annual contracts:** €50-200k.
- **Public-sector framework:** reserved-share contracts via Ausgleichsabgabe incentives.

## Market size (top-down)

- ~10 million people in Germany with a recognised disability (Schwerbehindertenausweis holders or equivalent).
- Reachable tester pool: 10-30k qualified testers over time.
- Buyer-side TAM: €30-80m across DACH in 3 years.

## Competitive landscape

- **Fable Tech (Canada):** dominant global player, English-centric.
- **Applause A11y:** enterprise but generic.
- **Pfennigparade München:** service organisation, not marketplace.
- **BIK, aleri:** consultancies, not marketplaces.

## Moat and differentiation

- German-language-first + DGS-first testing.
- Partnership with Pfennigparade, Lebenshilfe, DBSV as supply.
- Training programme that upgrades testers from session work to senior roles.
- Integration with BFSGPilot and other BFSG tools creates distribution.

## Regulatory path (RDG)

None. Labour-market and tech services.

## Key risks

- Supply-side scaling is slow and requires careful community trust.
- Fable expansion into DACH.
- Large inclusion companies (Pfennigparade) may build own version.

## MVP and first 90 days

1. Weeks 1-3: recruit 30 testers across four disability categories via Pfennigparade and DBSV.
2. Weeks 4-7: match with 5 paid test projects in Munich.
3. Weeks 8-12: run 3 continuous-panel pilots.

## Success KPIs

- Tester monthly earning (target: €1,000+ per active tester).
- Buyer-side NPS (target: 55+).
- Panel-retention at 3 months (target: 80%).
