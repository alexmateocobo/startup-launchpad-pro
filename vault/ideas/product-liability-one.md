# ProductLiabilityOne

**Pillar:** [[consumer-rights-awareness-and-enforcement]]
**One-liner:** Claim initiation platform for the new EU Product Liability Directive (2024/2853) covering software, AI systems and connected devices: victim intake, causation evidence assembly, and routing to plaintiff Kanzleien or VDuG qualified entities.
**Shortlist score:** 26/35
**Tags:** #b2c #platform #legal

## Problem

The new EU Product Liability Directive, in force from December 2024 and transposed by EU Member States by 9 December 2026, dramatically expands strict product liability to software, AI systems, digital services and connected products. Consumers will be able to claim damages for defective software updates, algorithmic decisions causing harm, and failed cybersecurity. But demonstrating defectiveness and causation is technically complex and expensive: the average consumer cannot compile the evidence pack required by courts. Plaintiff Kanzleien will need deal flow and evidence at scale. Qualified entities will want to run collective actions under VDuG.

## Regulatory driver / Why now

- EU PLD transposition deadline 9 December 2026; Germany's ProdHaftG revision in progress.
- AI Liability Directive still pending but moving.
- German Cybersecurity Act (BSIG) imposes software-security disclosure rules that create liability evidence.
- VDuG creates a class-redress backend.

## Target users

- **End users:** consumers harmed by defective software, smart-home devices, EV software, connected medical products, algorithmic decisions.
- **Payers:**
  - Plaintiff Kanzleien (deal flow).
  - Qualified entities (VDuG cases).
  - Litigation funders.
  - Consumer foundations for awareness-layer grants.

## Solution

A guided web platform where the consumer:

1. Describes the harm (e.g. OTA update bricked car, smart-meter billing error, wearable health misdiagnosis).
2. Uploads evidence (device logs, service records, screenshots, medical reports).
3. An AI-assisted triage engine classifies the claim per PLD and ProdHaftG.
4. Assembles an evidence pack with timeline, causation narrative, and damage quantification.
5. Routes to a matched plaintiff Kanzlei or a qualified entity running a VDuG case.

## Business model

- **Kanzlei referral:** flat €150-500 per qualified lead.
- **Success fee:** 15-25% of recovered amount for direct consumer path (RDG Inkasso).
- **Qualified-entity licence:** €30-75k/year for aggregated intake.
- **Insurer pre-audit:** €20-50k/year for product-liability carriers to triage claims.

## Market size (top-down)

- 82 million German consumers; EU 448 million.
- Estimated 200-400k viable PLD claims per year in Germany by 2028.
- TAM €50-120m in Germany; €400m+ EU.

## Competitive landscape

- **Myright:** vertical class-action player; narrow on diesel + data.
- **Classaction.de:** aggregator, not triage product.
- **US-style claim platforms (Marble, Classaction.org):** legal model doesn't fit EU.
- **No EU-native purpose-built PLD platform yet.**

## Moat and differentiation

- Evidence-assembly AI trained on product-defect patterns.
- Network effect between consumer intake, Kanzlei demand and funder capital.
- Regulatory credibility: strong ties to qualified entities.

## Regulatory path (RDG)

Dual track. Information + evidence assembly = out of RDG. Success-fee collection requires Inkassodienstleister licence. Kanzlei partnership for litigation.

## Key risks

- Transposition delays could slow the market.
- Courts may set high causation thresholds that reduce winnable share.
- Plaintiff-Kanzlei fragmentation makes channel management complex.
