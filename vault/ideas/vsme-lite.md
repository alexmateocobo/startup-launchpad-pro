# VSME Lite

**Pillar:** [[corporate-compliance-tooling]]
**One-liner:** Indirect-CSRD reporting platform for the ~200,000 German value-chain-cap suppliers pulled into Scope 3 data requests by DAX/MDAX and EU-listed customers, built on the EFRAG VSME voluntary standard and aligned with CSRD and CSDDD.
**Shortlist score:** 27/35
**Tags:** #b2b #saas #csrd #compliance

## Problem

CSRD applies to ~13,000 German companies directly from 2026 onward (wave 1: large companies; wave 2: listed SMEs; wave 3: non-EU parents). But the indirect effect is much larger: ~200,000 German suppliers face "data requests" from in-scope customers asking for ESG metrics (GHG emissions by scope, energy mix, water, social, governance). These suppliers — often Mittelstand with 20-300 FTE — cannot answer them. Customers then struggle to roll up Scope 3 data reliably, delaying their own reports. EFRAG released the Voluntary SME Standard (VSME) in December 2024 to standardise this request flow. Existing tooling (Normative, Greenly, Plan A, Coolset, Cozero) targets wave-1 direct reporters, not the indirect Mittelstand.

## Regulatory driver / Why now

- CSRD cascade through Scope 3 data requests.
- Corporate Sustainability Due Diligence Directive (CSDDD) adopted 2024, transposition 2026-2027.
- Lieferkettensorgfaltspflichtengesetz (LkSG) since 2023.
- EFRAG VSME standard finalised December 2024.
- CBAM (Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism) definitive phase from 2026.

## Target users

- **End users:** finance, sustainability, procurement leads at Mittelstand suppliers.
- **Payers:**
  - Mittelstand suppliers directly.
  - Large customers subsidising their supplier data request programmes.
  - Banks offering ESG-linked loans.
  - Industry associations (VDMA, ZVEI, VCI, BDI).

## Solution

A simple, template-driven SaaS:

1. Ingests customer data requests (usually from CDP, Sphera, EcoVadis, IntegrityNext).
2. Auto-generates VSME-format answers with minimal input.
3. Uses ERP/accounting connectors to pre-fill GHG, energy, waste.
4. Covers LkSG human-rights risk questionnaire.
5. Outputs: audit-ready dossier, machine-readable XBRL, customer-portal upload.

## Business model

- **Self-serve SaaS:** €99-499/month per supplier.
- **Customer-subsidised tier:** enterprise pays €5-20/seat for its supplier network.
- **Bank co-branded ESG tier:** free for SMEs with loans, bank pays €200-500/year.

## Market size (top-down)

- ~200,000 suppliers in German value chains.

## Competitive landscape

- **EcoVadis, IntegrityNext:** supply-chain-focused but complex and expensive.
- **Normative, Greenly, Plan A:** direct-reporter focus.
- **Cozero (Berlin):** carbon-first.
- **Nothing VSME-native and Mittelstand-affordable.**

## Moat and differentiation

- VSME-native templates, not retrofitted.
- Distribution via banks, VDMA and large-customer subsidy.
- Unique dataset of Scope 3 patterns in German value chains.

## Regulatory path (RDG)

None. ESG and accounting services.

## Key risks

- EcoVadis moves down-market aggressively.
- CSRD simplification debate could reduce scope and urgency.
- Data-quality liability if auditors reject reports.
