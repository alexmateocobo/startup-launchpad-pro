# DPO Copilot

**Pillar:** [[corporate-compliance-tooling]]
**One-liner:** Operational platform for external Data Protection Officer (DPO) firms serving the Mittelstand: records-of-processing, DPIA workflows, breach management, subject-access-request intake, and audit-ready dashboards — all GDPR + BDSG + EU Data Act aligned.
**Shortlist score:** 24/35
**Tags:** #b2b #saas #gdpr #compliance

## Problem

In Germany any company with 20+ employees routinely processing personal data must appoint a Data Protection Officer. ~200,000 companies are in scope; most outsource to external DPO firms (~2,500 such firms nationwide). These firms run on Excel, Notion, and OneTrust (which is too expensive and enterprise-focused for their customer mix). Workload is rising: EU Data Act (September 2025), BFSG interaction with accessibility data, DORA for financial-services DPOs, CSRD governance disclosures, and an increasing number of subject-access and Article 82 damages requests. DPO firms want a platform tuned to their delivery economics, not a €30k/year enterprise suite.

## Regulatory driver / Why now

- EU Data Act (active for B2B and B2C data sharing from 12 September 2025).
- BfDI and Länder-Datenschutzbehörden ramping inspection capacity.
- Article 82 DSGVO damages claim volume rising.
- DORA and NIS2 impose new DPO obligations in financial services.
- DataGuard and HeyData consolidating the mid-market; white space for a DPO-firm-native tool.

## Target users

- **End users:** external DPO firms serving 20-500 Mittelstand customers each.
- **Payers:**
  - DPO firms (SaaS seats + per-customer pricing).
  - Inhouse-DPO teams at larger SMEs.
  - DAV Arbeitsgemeinschaft IT-Recht members.

## Solution

1. Multi-tenant workbench: one DPO firm, many client organisations.
2. Records of Processing (Verzeichnis von Verarbeitungstätigkeiten, VVT) builder with industry templates.
3. DPIA automator using LLM for draft narrative.
4. Breach timer + workflow (72h notification).
5. Subject-access-request intake + automated response drafting.
6. Audit dashboards + Datenschutzbehörden-ready evidence packs.
7. Knowledge base linking CJEU, BGH, OLG rulings by industry.

## Business model

- **DPO firm SaaS:** €199-999/month per DPO user + €10-30/month per end client.
- **Enterprise inhouse-DPO:** €25-75k/year.
- **Marketplace tier:** charge end clients a shared licence for SAR intake portal.

## Market size (top-down)

- ~2,500 DPO firms in DACH, each serving 20-500 clients.
- Reachable ARR: €15-30m within 3 years.

## Competitive landscape

- **OneTrust, TrustArc:** enterprise but oversized.
- **DataGuard, HeyData:** serve Mittelstand directly, compete with DPO firms rather than equip them.
- **Proliance DataGuard, ISICO, Audatis Manager:** German incumbents, showing age.
- **Ethyca, Transcend:** US-first, weak German-law coverage.

## Moat and differentiation

- DPO-firm-native economics: multi-tenant + per-client pricing.
- German-law-first knowledge base.
- Integration with DataGuard ecosystem possible as a channel.

## Regulatory path (RDG)

None. Operational tooling for licensed DPOs.

## Key risks

- DataGuard extends down to serve DPO firms.
- DPO-firm market is fragmented and price-sensitive.
- Data Act and DORA may consolidate around a single large platform.
