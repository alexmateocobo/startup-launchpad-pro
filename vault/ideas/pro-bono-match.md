# EnforcingCitizensRights

**Pillar:** [[enforcing-citizens-rights]]
**One-liner:** A two-sided marketplace that turns the Beratungshilfeschein (German legal aid voucher) into a one-click match with a pro bono or legal-aid lawyer, white-labelled for consumer protection NGOs.
**Shortlist score:** 26/35

## Problem

Germany has two well-designed legal-aid schemes that are chronically under-used:

- **Beratungshilfe (advisory aid):** €15 consumer copay, lawyer paid by the state; issued by the local court.
- **Prozesskostenhilfe (legal aid for proceedings):** state covers lawyer and court costs.

Yet Handbook Germany, Caritas, Diakonie and Verbraucherzentralen field thousands of requests per year with no structured way to route them. Citizens who receive a Beratungsschein often struggle to find a lawyer willing to accept it because the RVG fee is low and administrative load is high. This creates a market failure at the precise point where access to justice is most needed.

## Regulatory driver / Why now

- 2025 asset allowance for Prozesskostenhilfe set at €10,000 plus €500 per dependent — expanding eligibility.
- The Federal Ministry of Justice is piloting the Digitale Rechtsantragsstelle to improve access to legal information.
- EU Directive on representative actions has raised civil-society expectations for digital consumer support.

## Target users

- **End users:** low-income citizens, refugees, single parents, migrants with a Beratungsschein.
- **Payers:**
  - Foundations and NGOs (Pro Bono Deutschland, Deutscher Anwaltverein Foundation, Stiftung Rechtsstaat).
  - Public sector: Städtetag, Landkreistag, Justizministerien pay for digital intake rails.
  - Kanzleien that want to fulfil pro bono obligations or acquire socially-responsible brand value.

## Solution

A web and mobile app that:

1. Helps citizens determine Beratungshilfe and Prozesskostenhilfe eligibility via guided triage.
2. Generates the application form for the Beratungsschein at the local court.
3. After issuance, matches the Schein to a participating lawyer within 48 hours based on legal domain, language and location.
4. Tracks the case lifecycle and collects anonymised outcome data for foundations and ministries.

NGO partners get a white-labelled portal branded to their community.

## Business model

- **NGO licence:** €5-15k/year for the white-label portal with SLAs.
- **Public-sector SaaS:** €25-100k/year per Bundesland justice ministry.
- **Lawyer-side premium:** optional €29-79/month subscription to Kanzleien for case matching and streamlined billing.

## Market size (top-down)

- Approximately 700,000 Beratungsscheine and Prozesskostenhilfe applications per year in Germany.
- 16 Justizministerien, 400+ Verbraucherzentralen and allied NGOs as addressable accounts.
- Reachable TAM: €20-40m/year combining public sector, NGO licences and lawyer subscriptions.

## Competitive landscape

- **Handbook Germany:** strong information layer, no matching.
- **Anwalt.de, Advocado:** generic lawyer marketplaces; not tuned to Beratungshilfe economics.
- **Caritas, Diakonie:** strong distribution, lack digital product.

## Moat and differentiation

- Trust: citizens will not trust a for-profit intermediary; NGO white-label solves this.
- Outcome data: anonymised dataset on legal-aid caseloads is publication-grade for ministries and foundations.
- Lawyer supply: a curated panel with streamlined RVG billing closes the fee-pain problem that keeps lawyers off the scheme.

## Regulatory path (RDG)

Information and matching only, no legal advice. Lawyers remain RA-licensed. Clean RDG path.

## Key risks

- Long sales cycles with ministries and NGOs.
- Lawyer supply: paying a network of lawyers to participate may require seed-stage subsidy.
- Bundesland variation in Beratungshilfe administration.

## MVP and first 90 days

1. Weeks 1-3: partner with one Verbraucherzentrale (Bayern) and one Caritas diocese.
2. Weeks 4-7: guided triage flow plus Beratungsschein generator in one Bundesland.
3. Weeks 8-12: match-making pilot with 20 participating lawyers from the Munich bar; 100 cases end to end.

## Success KPIs

- Time from request to matched lawyer (target: under 48 hours).
- Percentage of citizens who reach a resolution (target: 75%).
- Cost per successful match for the public partner (target: under €50).
