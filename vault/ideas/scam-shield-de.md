# ScamShield DE

**Pillar:** [[consumer-rights-awareness-and-enforcement]]
**One-liner:** Victim-side platform for online fraud, phishing and deepfake scams: fast-track chargeback, freezing bank-account requests to providers, counter-demand against unjustified Inkasso, and fraud-pattern intelligence shared with law enforcement.
**Shortlist score:** 24/35

## Problem

Germany's Bundeskriminalamt (BKA) recorded ~130,000 cases of Cybercrime im engeren Sinne in 2024; the clear majority are online fraud, phishing, deepfake-initiated transfers and illegitimate Inkasso demands. Victims face a bewildering process: file with Polizei, notify the bank, dispute with the merchant, respond to threatening Inkasso letters, report to Verbraucherzentrale. Most victims do none of the above or only a fragment. Reputable Inkasso firms (Pair Finance, Intrum) are also weaponised by fraudsters to send fake demands. Financial and emotional losses compound.

## Regulatory driver / Why now

- PSD2 strong customer authentication obligations create strict timelines for chargeback and fraud claims.
- Digital Operational Resilience Act (DORA) raises banks' obligations to handle fraud-victim requests swiftly from 2025.
- Germany's 2025 Hinweisgeberschutzgesetz enforcement spillover raises public expectation of organised response.
- Verbraucherzentrale launched a 2025 joint programme with the BKA on fraud awareness.

## Target users

- **End users:** victims of online fraud, phishing, investment scams, love scams, business-email compromise, unauthorised Inkasso.
- **Payers:**
  - End users (pay-per-case, low ticket).
  - Banks and payment-service providers (complaint-handling insourcing).
  - Consumer protection foundations.

## Solution

A guided web and mobile app:

1. Categorises the fraud pattern within minutes.
2. Generates the set of fast-track actions: bank chargeback request, BaFin complaint, BKA online report, merchant dispute, Verbraucherzentrale notification.
3. For unjustified Inkasso: drafts a Widerspruch and escalates to the Rechtsdienstleistungsregister if the Inkasso provider is non-registered.
4. Crowdsourced fraud-pattern intelligence becomes a shared database for banks and law enforcement.

## Business model

- **Consumer:** €19-49 per case or subscription (€4.99/month family plan).
- **Bank and PSP licence:** €50-300k/year for fraud-intake integration.
- **Foundation grants** for the awareness layer.

## Market size (top-down)

- 3-5 million household-level online-fraud incidents per year in Germany (BKA + underreported estimate).
- TAM estimated at €50-120m between direct consumer and B2B layers.

## Competitive landscape

- **Trusted Shops:** e-commerce trust layer, not fraud response.
- **Chargeback companies (Chargebacks911, Ethoca):** merchant-side.
- **Bank apps:** fragmented fraud handling; poor UX; pre-built integration opportunity.

## Moat and differentiation

- Breadth across bank, merchant, Inkasso and law-enforcement rails.
- Shared fraud-pattern intelligence grows with each case.
- Consumer-trust brand is defensible relative to generic chatbots.

## Regulatory path (RDG)

Information and form-automation only. Any Inkasso counter-activity routed to a partner Kanzlei. Clean RDG path.

## Key risks

- Banks may preempt the opportunity by improving their own fraud workflows.
- Quality control: wrong fraud classification can harm real victims.
- Regulatory scrutiny on data-sharing with law enforcement.

## MVP and first 90 days

1. Weeks 1-3: interview 20 recent fraud victims and 5 bank complaint-handling managers.
2. Weeks 4-7: build the phishing / fake-Inkasso pathway for Sparkasse customers.
3. Weeks 8-12: run a closed beta with 100 victims; measure recovery rate and user NPS.

## Success KPIs

- Time from first contact to bank-intervention request (target: under 24 hours).
- Percentage of claims recovered in full or in part (target: 45%).
- Share of unique fraud patterns contributed to intelligence dataset.
