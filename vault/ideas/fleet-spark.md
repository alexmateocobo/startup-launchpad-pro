# FleetSpark

**Pillar:** [[ev-charging-infrastructure]]
**One-liner:** Fleet electrification planning and compliance platform that combines GEIG charging-infrastructure obligations, §14a EnWG grid-fee optimisation, and EU CO₂ fleet targets into one Fuhrpark-manager dashboard.
**Shortlist score:** 30/35
**Tags:** #b2b #saas #ev #compliance

## Problem

German Fuhrpark managers (corporate fleet managers) face a convergence of three simultaneous compliance pressures with no integrated tool: (1) GEIG requires their company parking to have charging points by 2025; (2) §14a EnWG requires registration of every charger and enables grid-fee savings; (3) EU Regulation 2019/631 sets mandatory CO₂ fleet-average targets that make EV adoption not optional but legally required. Each of these regulations has its own reporting format, its own authority, and its own deadline. Fleet managers currently handle each with a separate spreadsheet and a different consultant. The 4.5 million company cars in Germany represent the fastest electrification lever available — and the most administratively neglected.

## Regulatory driver / Why now

- GEIG parking deadlines: January 2025 (existing non-residential) and ongoing for new builds.
- §14a EnWG: registrations mandatory since January 2024, most fleets unregistered.
- EU Regulation 2019/631 CO₂ targets: zero-emission new cars from 2035; intermediate 2025 targets already active.
- EU "Greening Corporate Fleets" regulation in preparation (Commission publication expected late 2025), extending mandatory ZEV purchase quotas to corporate fleets.

## Target users

- **End users:** Fuhrpark managers, CFOs, sustainability managers at companies with 50+ company cars.
- **Payers:**
  - SME fleet (50–200 cars): €500–1,500/month.
  - Large fleet (200–2,000 cars): €2,000–8,000/month.
  - Fleet leasing companies (Alphabet, LeasePlan, Arval) as channel partners.

## Solution

A unified fleet electrification compliance platform that:
1. Imports current fleet data (CSV, or API from leasing companies).
2. Maps vehicles to parking locations and generates a GEIG compliance gap analysis per site.
3. Calculates current CO₂ fleet average vs EU targets; projects the EV-adoption rate needed to comply.
4. Automates §14a registration for all chargers at company sites.
5. Generates Netzanschlussantrag pre-fills per site and tracks grid-connection status.
6. Produces a board-ready electrification roadmap with capex scenarios.

## Business model

- **Tiered SaaS** by fleet size.
- **Leasing-company white-label:** €30–80k/year for integration into fleet management portals.
- **Consulting upsell:** grid-connection project management at day-rate.

## Market size (top-down)

- ~200,000 German companies with a meaningful company-car fleet (5+ vehicles).
- Of which ~15,000 have 50+ cars and meaningful compliance budget.
- At €18k average ACV and 5% penetration: SAM €13.5m; TAM €100m+ including EU fleet operators.

## Competitive landscape

- **Twaice, Jedlix, Monta:** EV charging optimisation but no fleet-wide compliance layer.
- **Fleet management software (Vimcar, Webfleet, FleetComplete):** vehicle tracking, no GEIG/CO₂ compliance.
- **Consulting firms (Deloitte, Roland Berger):** advisory only, expensive, not scalable.

## Moat and differentiation

- Multi-regulation integration (GEIG + §14a + CO₂ fleet) in one workflow is not replicable quickly.
- Leasing-company channel creates a structural distribution advantage.
- Fleet electrification timeline data (which car replaced by which EV, when) becomes a proprietary dataset.

## Regulatory path (RDG)

No RDG exposure: fleet planning and compliance reporting is operational, not legal-services work.

## Key risks

- Leasing companies build this in-house for their own customers.
- EU fleet-CO₂ regulation is relaxed under lobbying pressure from automakers.
- §14a savings are too small relative to fleet budgets to be a decision driver.
