# GEIGPilot

**Pillar:** [[ev-charging-infrastructure]]
**One-liner:** Compliance and project-management SaaS for building owners and Hausverwaltungen to meet Germany's GEIG charging-point obligations before the €10,000 fine clock starts.
**Shortlist score:** 29/35

## Problem

The Gebäude-Elektromobilitätsinfrastruktur-Gesetz (GEIG) required all non-residential buildings with more than 20 parking spaces to have at least one operational charging point by 1 January 2025. From 2025 onward, every new non-residential building with at least 7 parking spaces must have one charging point and pre-wiring for every third space. By June 2026, Germany must transpose the EU's updated Energy Performance of Buildings Directive (EPBD), which extends obligations to residential buildings with more than 3 parking spaces. Fines of up to €10,000 per building apply. Yet most Hausverwaltungen, Bauträger, and property owners have no structured way to track which properties are compliant, what installations are needed, or how to obtain a Netzanschluss. They rely on ad-hoc installer quotes and Word documents.

## Regulatory driver / Why now

- GEIG compliance deadline for existing non-residential buildings was 1 January 2025 — many are already in breach.
- EPBD transposition due June 2026 will add a second, larger wave of residential obligations.
- Germany's Masterplan Charging Infrastructure 2030 (Oct 2025) commits to revising GEIG and tightening enforcement.
- Fines of up to €10,000 per property; Bundesländer building-supervision authorities beginning spot checks.

## Target users

- **End users:** Hausverwaltungen (property managers), Gebäudeeigentümer (commercial landlords), Bauträger (developers).
- **Payers:**
  - Hausverwaltungen managing 20+ properties: €200–600/month SaaS.
  - Large property funds (Vonovia, LEG, Patrizia): €20–80k/year enterprise licence.
  - Installer networks as channel partners (ENGIE, Goldbeck, Streif Baulogistik).

## Solution

A property-compliance SaaS that:
1. Ingests a property portfolio (CSV or API from existing software like Domus, Haufe or GFAD).
2. Scores each property against current GEIG thresholds and upcoming EPBD rules.
3. Generates a prioritised remediation queue with cost estimates.
4. Produces GEIG-compliant documentation for building-permit applications and installer briefings.
5. Tracks Netzanschluss applications per property, with automated reminders.
6. Generates an audit-ready evidence pack for regulatory inspections.

## Business model

- **Tiered SaaS:** €99/month per property manager (up to 10 properties); €299/month (up to 50); €799/month (unlimited).
- **Installer marketplace commission:** 3–5% on jobs sourced through the platform.
- **EPBD readiness audit packs:** €1,500–5,000 per portfolio assessment.

## Market size (top-down)

- ~3M non-residential buildings in Germany; ~200k with more than 20 parking spaces are immediately subject to GEIG.
- ~27,000 professional Hausverwaltungen manage the bulk of the portfolio.
- At €3,600 average ACV per Hausverwaltung and 10% penetration: SAM €10m; TAM €50m+ including EPBD residential wave and EU transpositions.

## Competitive landscape

- **QPLIX / Yardi / Domus:** general property-management tools with no GEIG module.
- **peter-park, ChargePoint, ENGIE e-mobility:** installer-centric; no compliance workflow.
- **Legaltech advisories (KPMG-Law, Gleiss Lutz):** advisory-only, expensive.
- No dedicated GEIG compliance SaaS exists as of mid-2026.

## Moat and differentiation

- Growing database of per-property compliance history across a portfolio creates switching cost.
- Installer-network integration creates a closed loop from compliance gap to completed installation.
- EPBD 2026 wave locks in customers who already have the platform before the next deadline.

## Regulatory path (RDG)

No RDG exposure: the product provides building-compliance project management, not legal advice. Document templates should carry a disclaimer that legal interpretation requires a qualified lawyer, preserving the clean RDG position.

## Key risks

- Enforcement remains lax; owners ignore fines.
- Installer capacity constraints delay installations and reduce platform value.
- Large property-software incumbents (Domus, Haufe) add a GEIG module.

## MVP and first 90 days

1. Weeks 1–3: interview 10 Hausverwaltungen + 3 Bundesland building-supervision offices.
2. Weeks 4–7: build portfolio importer, GEIG threshold scorer, and evidence-pack generator; pilot with 2 Munich Hausverwaltungen.
3. Weeks 8–12: deliver GEIG status reports for 30 properties; close first paid pilot contract.

## Success KPIs

- Evidence packs generated per pilot (target: 5 per Hausverwaltung).
- Signed pilots at week 12 (target: 3 Hausverwaltungen).
