# RentGuardian

**Pillar:** [[consumer-rights-awareness-and-enforcement]]
**One-liner:** Expat-first next-generation Conny: Mietpreisbremse checks, Nebenkostenabrechnung audit, Kündigungsverteidigung, and Mieterhöhung defence — in English, German and Spanish — for Munich, Berlin, Hamburg, Frankfurt and the Top-10 cities.
**Shortlist score:** 26/35

## Problem

Conny (wenigermiete.de) built a pioneering Mietpreisbremse product but remains German-language-first, Berlin-centric, and focused on the initial rent-setting. Expats and international renters — who make up >25% of tenants in Munich and Frankfurt — face three other high-pain problems that Conny does not solve well:

1. **Nebenkostenabrechnung:** 50-80% of annual service-cost statements contain errors. Expats almost never challenge them.
2. **Mieterhöhung:** landlords frequently push increases that exceed Kappungsgrenze or ignore Mietspiegel.
3. **Kündigung:** Eigenbedarfskündigung and other termination types require precise, time-sensitive defence.

Existing alternatives — Mieterbund membership, wenigermiete.de, individual Mieterschutz Kanzleien — are either language-limited, narrow in scope, or expensive.

## Regulatory driver / Why now

- Mietpreisbremse extended to 2029 in the Federal Coalition Agreement.
- New rental legislation coming in 2026 for indexed leases (Indexmiete) oversight.
- Higher cost of living means even modest Nebenkostenabrechnung refunds are material to tenants.
- Online-Zivilverfahren opens a new filing channel under €10,000 that covers most tenancy disputes.

## Target users

- **End users:** expats and international renters in Munich, Berlin, Hamburg, Frankfurt, Stuttgart, Düsseldorf, Köln.
- **Payers:**
  - Tenants (success fee or flat fee).
  - Corporate relocation providers.
  - Rechtsschutzversicherer (Arag, Roland, Allianz).
  - Bar association cooperation with partner Kanzleien.

## Solution

A mobile app (English-first, German and Spanish second) that supports four flows:

1. **Mietpreisbremse check:** upload lease + Mietspiegel match → refund claim.
2. **Nebenkostenabrechnung audit:** upload the statement → AI parses and checks heating, water, administrative costs.
3. **Mieterhöhung defence:** upload the Mieterhöhungsverlangen → auto-check Kappungsgrenze + Mietspiegel.
4. **Kündigung defence:** upload the Kündigung → validity check + drafted rebuttal.

## Business model

- **Success fee (Inkasso):** 25-33% for refunds and rent reductions.
- **Flat fee:** €99-249 for defence drafts and audits.
- **Corporate B2B:** €10-30/unit/year for relocation / property-management white-label.

## Market size (top-down)

- ~24 million rental households in Germany; ~5 million in the Top-10 cities.
- At €40 blended ARPU and 2% household penetration, reachable TAM of €4-8m in Top-10 cities.
- Expansion to all of Germany triples the figure.

## Competitive landscape

- **Conny:** strong brand but German-language and Berlin-centric.
- **Mieterbund and Mieterverein:** membership organisations with strong legal backing but poor digital UX.
- **Wohnsinn, ImmoScout:** listing and tenancy tools, no dispute features.

## Moat and differentiation

- Multi-language-first UX.
- Four-flow breadth beats Conny's Mietpreisbremse-first scope.
- Integration with partner Kanzlei network for escalation.
- Dataset of Nebenkostenabrechnung errors is proprietary and highly marketable.

## Regulatory path (RDG)

Inkassodienstleister registration required for success-fee model. BGH 2019 Lexfox/wenigermiete.de ruling sets the precedent. Flat-fee tier operates under information-only position.

## Key risks

- Conny moves into expat segment.
- Landlord associations push regulatory narrowing of Mietpreisbremse.
- OCR of heterogeneous Nebenkostenabrechnung templates is non-trivial.

## MVP and first 90 days

1. Weeks 1-3: interview 20 Munich expats who paid rent in the last 12 months.
2. Weeks 4-7: Nebenkostenabrechnung parser for the two largest Hausverwaltung templates (ista, Techem style).
3. Weeks 8-12: handle 30 real cases end to end; measure refund delta.

## Success KPIs

- Completion rate of lease-upload-to-refund (target: 70%).
- Referral share from corporate relocation channel (target: 25% of volume).
