# RobotPermit

**Pillar:** [[autonomous-and-robotic-mobility]]
**One-liner:** City-by-city permit automation SaaS for sidewalk delivery robot operators navigating Germany's fragmented §70 StVO Ausnahmegenehmigung regime.
**Shortlist score:** 23/35 · #watchlist
**Tags:** #b2b #saas #compliance #mobility

## Problem

Delivery robots operating on German pavements (Gehwegrobots) — such as those deployed by Starship Technologies, Cartken (Google-backed), and Heinemann — must obtain an Ausnahmegenehmigung from the local Straßenverkehrsbehörde under §70 StVO for each operating area, as no national framework yet exists. Each Straßenverkehrsbehörde has its own requirements: some require a formal Technikgutachten (technical expert opinion), others a liability-insurance certificate with minimum coverage of €10M, others a specific operating-concept document. Operators expanding from one city to five cities face five separate and non-standardised permit processes. The UK, US, and Estonia have established national frameworks; Germany has been legislating a national robot ordinance (in deliberation since 2022) but has not yet enacted one.

## Regulatory driver / Why now

- §70 StVO Ausnahmegenehmigung is the current legal basis; it creates city-by-city fragmentation but is legally clear.
- German national robot ordinance expected in legislative pipeline 2026–2027 — when passed, it will standardise requirements and create a one-time national registration opportunity.
- Logistics operators (DHL, Rewe Group, Edeka) actively piloting delivery robots; regulatory navigation is the stated bottleneck.

## Target users

- **End users:** regulatory and expansion teams at delivery robot operators.
- **Payers:**
  - Starship Technologies, Cartken, Heinemann: €800–2,000/month per 5-city tier.
  - Logistics companies piloting robots: per-city permit package.

## Solution

A permit-management platform that:
1. Maintains a database of §70 StVO requirements per city (coverage, documentation, timelines).
2. Generates city-specific Ausnahmegenehmigung applications pre-populated with operator data.
3. Manages Technikgutachten scheduling with certified experts (TÜV, DEKRA).
4. Tracks permit validity, renewal dates, and operating-area boundaries.
5. When the national ordinance is enacted: automigrates permits to the national framework.

## Business model

- **Subscription SaaS** per city in operation.
- **Permit-package consulting:** fixed fee per city for first application.
- **National ordinance readiness pack:** one-time product when federal framework launches.

## Market size (top-down)

- ~10 active delivery robot operators in Germany; ~30 logistics companies piloting.
- At €15k average ACV and 50% operator penetration: SAM €225k short-term; TAM grows substantially when national ordinance creates a registration market for thousands of robots.

## Competitive landscape

- No dedicated delivery-robot permit SaaS exists in Germany.
- Regulatory affairs consultancies handle this manually.

## Moat and differentiation

- City-ordinance database is proprietary; national-ordinance readiness positions the platform as the migration tool.
- TÜV/DEKRA expert network for Technikgutachten is a partnership that takes time to build.

## Regulatory path (RDG)

Moderate RDG risk: preparing Ausnahmegenehmigung applications borders on legal-document preparation. Mitigation: present as a template tool; partner with a transport-law firm for operating-concept sign-off.

## Key risks

- Market too small before the national ordinance; difficult to sustain before then.
- National ordinance creates a simple online registration that operators handle themselves.
- Delivery robot adoption in Germany stalls due to pavement-sharing conflicts.

3. Weeks 8–12: process 5 pilot permit applications; track through approval.
